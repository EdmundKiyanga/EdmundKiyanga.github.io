About GGT

Good Governance Tanzania (GGT) is a Non-Government, non-profit making and a politically non-partisan organization. It was first registered on April 11, 2008 under certificate of incorporation number 3405 as Good Governance Trust (GGT). The name was changed to Good Governance Tanzania (GGT) and registered on January 7, 2014 under certificate of compliance number 00001555.

Read More

Objectives

The principal objectives of Good Governance Tanzania are:

  • To promote and advocate for good and effective leadership, good governance and human rights.
  • To fight corruption in public service and all types of inhuman behavior in society; such as human trafficking, drug abuse, child labor, killing of albinos, etc.
  • To advocate for protection of the environment to enable the planet continue to support life.
  • To promote education and training at all levels of society in leadership, good governance and anti-corruption.
  • To facilitate and promote growth of social sciences and effective management of resource for development.
  • To fight against sexual abuse, murders of albinos and the elderly, female circumcision and all types of discrimination.
  • To work towards empowerment, promotion, and protection of rights of women and youth.
  • To work towards the empowerment, promotion and protection of community health and wellbeing of vulnerable groups in society.




MissionStatement

Good Governance Tanzania (GGT) is dedicated to promoting highest quality leadership, good governance, democracy, protection of the environment and national resource and fighting corruption through consultancy, research, education, training and publishing to create a better, safer and healthier life for all Tanzanians in the 21 century and beyond.

VisionAlignment

We aim to be the most respected institution in promoting good and effective leadership, good governance and anti-corruption in Africa for social and economic development and peace on the continent.

Projects

No. Project Name Project Date Description Purpose
1. KUJIAMINI 2010 This project was conducted in Morogoro Region at Bigwa girl secondary school. The project involved more than 50 participants from different schools in Morogoro region, including Bigwa girls secondary school. Purpose of the project was to develop self confidence among girls and inspire/encouraged them to take leaderships position in the community. Participants were told how to build up their self esteem and confidence
2. GOOD LEADERSHIP 2015 This project was conducted during the period of campaigns and election. The project involved participants from 10 regions which includes Dar es Salaam, Musoma ,Morogoro, Mbeya, Dodoma, Tanga , Arusha, Mwanza , Kigoma and Mtwara. It was conducted with the purpose of training young leaders from different political parties who were involves in election in different positions in their parties. The project mainly focused in training foundations of effective leadership. Honesty, wisdom, courage, confidence and sincerity are the best qualities of a good leader. A good leader will be able to resist/fight corruption and do the right thing even no one is watching and not to tolerate wrong doing in others. Develop strongest skills of analyzing problems, and courage of thinking broadly, with the ability of working as a team and good communication skills. To be able to focus and have a strong commitment to their goals (vision, objectives and dreams), strong faith and belief in what they are doing and towards people they are doing it with. Also to be able to produce more leaders by coaching, supporting, teaching, giving advice or hints, rewarding and sometimes punishing and testing
3. VICOBA 2015 Good Governance Tanzania has been working with Ahadiway life Development Initiative for the period of two years in the area of management. In this regard we participated in forming sixty three (63) VICOBA groups in Musoma and Shinyanga and 23 in Dar es Salaam, building capacity and strengthening VICOBA groups and monitoring and evaluating the financial capacity of members of the VICOBA groups.
