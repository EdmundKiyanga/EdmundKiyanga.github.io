About GGT

Good Governance Tanzania (GGT) is a Non-Government, non-profit making and a politically non-partisan organization. It was first registered on April 11, 2008 under certificate of incorporation number 3405 as Good Governance Trust (GGT). The name was changed to Good Governance Tanzania (GGT) and registered on January 7, 2014 under certificate of compliance number 00001555.

The founder members of GGT are: Mr. Jesse Bahati Mashimi, Ms. Theresia Gyunda, Dr. Vincent Bernard Kihiyo and Mr. Francis H. Kugesha Their brief CVs are shown bellow. The reason for establishing Good Governance Tanzania (GGT) was address the challenges facing Tanzania with regard to leadership, democracy, good governance and economic development. Tanzania is gifted with many natural resources, such as the sea, lakes, rivers, game reserves and wild animals, ruby, tanzanite, gold, natural gas, diamonds, uranium, etc. On top of that Tanzanians are patient, friendly, polite, non-violent peace loving people. The country has also enjoyed peace since independence more than fifty years ago, and changed leaders peacefully through the ballot box rather than by the barrel of a gun like some other African countries. But all these assets have not helped Tanzania to move from poverty to prosperity as one would expect and Tanzanians are among the poorest people on the planet.

We believe the problem is poor leadership. Tanzania has no clear system which enables leaders to come out with a common standing about leadership and national interests. Leaders get into power without a clear idea of what they are required to do, make decisions according to what they believe is right given the culture of their tribes and there is no common understanding among themselves and the people of leadership and decision making that can solve the problems of poverty and bring about scientific, technological and economic development.



Leadership Challenges Facing Tanzania

Political, economic and social developments are possible only where there is effective leadership and good governance. Among other competences, effective leaders have vision, courage to make tough and sometimes unpopular decisions, empathy to see issues from the point of view of the people and ability to inspire them to higher ideals.

Good governance is the use of power at all levels of society: from the family, community, national to global level in economic and social interests and survival of society and individuals, and effective management of resources. It requires leaders to act in the interests of the people; and not in their selfish personal interests. It requires them to use their power to develop policies that promote and safeguard the interests of the people, put in place a legal framework that recognizes the legal and property rights of individuals and groups, and protect the rights of the people by enforcing the law equitably.

Due to the challenges Tanzania is facing in effective leadership, Good Governance Tanzania (GGT) was established with the purpose of advocating and promoting effective leadership and good governance by addressing those problems through training, consultancy, research and publication and dissemination of research findings to ensure resources are harnessed and used effectively to enable Tanzanians live a long, happy and prosperous life. We training present leaders and those aspiring to be leaders, in particular the youth, conducting research and providing consultancy to institutions on leadership, democracy, protection of the environment, public service ethics, good governance, human rights and business management.
